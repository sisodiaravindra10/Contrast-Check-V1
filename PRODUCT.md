# Contrast Check

## Product Purpose

A one-page web artifact arguing that dark mode is currently too dark for the brightness levels real devices actually run at. Demonstrates six middle-ground themes calibrated to pass WCAG AAA while staying legible at 30-50% screen brightness, with a live brightness simulator and a side-by-side comparison against true dark and true light.

Inspired by the high-contrast warmth of the Nokia 3310's amber LCD, the calibrated frontlight of the Kindle Paperwhite, and the original Solarized scheme by Ethan Schoonover.

## Users

A product person showing their CEO why their team should ship a calibrated dark mode instead of the SaaS default. Single use case: a persuasive demo opened in a browser on a MacBook in a half-dark conference room.

Secondary: designers and developers who want to copy theme tokens for their own dark mode work.

## Register

brand — the design IS the product. This is a pitch artifact, not a tool that serves a separate workflow.

## Brand Voice

- Three brand-voice words: warm, precise, revelatory.
- Direct, opinionated. Sentences carry the argument; no marketing fluff.
- The interface practices what it preaches. The shell uses a warm middle-ground theme. The argument is the design.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages with hero metric cards.
- Editorial-magazine reflex (display serif italic, drop caps, ruled separators).
- Crypto/AI neon-on-pitch-black aesthetics.
- The Vercel/Linear monochrome-glass aesthetic.

## Strategic Principles

1. The shell embodies one of the themes it is teaching. The page is the proof.
2. Real contrast numbers. Computed live from OKLCH, not labeled "passes AA" without showing the math.
3. The brightness slider is the demo. Side-by-side comparison is the punchline.
4. Single HTML file, no build. Email-attachable, share-link-able, archive-stable.
